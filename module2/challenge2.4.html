LET'S DO SOME COOKING

Cooking isn’t just about recipes—it’s about adding a touch of love to every dish. When we cook with care and joy, our food becomes more than just a meal; it becomes a gift.
So, as you embark on this culinary journey, remember: the secret ingredient is always love. Let it guide you, and your dishes will become something truly special.!

  1. Afang Soup

    Afang soup is a rich, flavorful dish from southern Nigeria, celebrated for its unique blend of Afang (wild spinach) and waterleaf, combined with tender meats, seafood, and spices. This hearty, nutrient-packed soup is a staple of Nigerian cuisine, bringing warmth and a taste of tradition to every meal.

    • Cook the Meat and Kpomo: In a pot, add the meat, kpomo, seasoning cubes, and a little water. Let it simmer until the meat and kpomo are tender.
    • Add Waterleaf and Periwinkle: Once the meat is cooked, add the waterleaf and periwinkle to the pot. Stir and allow them to cook until the waterleaf releases its juices.
    • Add Crayfish and Pepper: Add the ground crayfish and pepper to the mixture. Stir well and cook for a few minutes to blend the flavors.
    • Add Afang Leaf and Red Oil: Finally, add the Afang leaf and pour in a generous amount of red oil. Stir everything together, cover, and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes on low heat. Adjust seasoning to taste.
    • Serve Hot: Afang Soup is best enjoyed hot, served with fufu, eba, or your favorite side!

  2. Bitterleaf Soup

    Bitterleaf soup, or Ofe Onugbu, is a beloved Nigerian dish known for its rich, savory taste and subtle bitterness. Perfect with fufu or pounded yam, it's a comforting and flavorful meal.

    • Cook the Meats and Stockfish: In a large pot, place the assorted meats, kpomo, and stockfish. Add seasoning cubes, a pinch of salt, and enough water to cover. Cook until the meats are tender.
    • Add Dried Fish and Bitterleaf: Once the meats are cooked, add the dried fish and bitterleaf. If using fresh bitterleaf, wash it thoroughly to remove most of the bitterness before adding. Let it cook for another 5-10 minutes.
    • Add Palm Oil and Cocoyam Paste: Add a generous amount of palm oil for color and flavor, then add the cocoyam paste (or egusi) to thicken the soup. Stir well to mix everything.
    • Add Crayfish and Pepper: Stir in the ground crayfish and pepper. Let it simmer for about 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to blend and the soup to thicken. Adjust seasoning as needed.
    • Serve and Enjoy: Bitterleaf Soup is best served hot with fufu, pounded yam, or your preferred swallow.

  3. Oha Soup

    Oha soup is a traditional Nigerian delicacy from the Igbo tribe, loved for its rich, earthy flavors and tender Oha leaves. This hearty soup, made with cocoyam as a thickener and a blend of meats and spices, is perfect with fufu or pounded yam, bringing comfort and warmth to every meal.

    • Cook the Meats and Stockfish: In a pot, add the assorted meats, kpomo, and stockfish with seasoning cubes, salt, and enough water to cover. Boil until tender.
    • Add Palm Oil and Cocoyam Paste: Add palm oil to the pot, then stir in the cocoyam paste or achi powder to thicken the soup. Stir well and let it cook for about 5-10 minutes to blend.
    • Add Crayfish, Pepper, and Dried Fish: Add the ground crayfish, pepper, and dried fish. Stir, cover, and simmer for a few more minutes for the flavors to meld.
    • Add Oha Leaves Last: Tear the Oha leaves into pieces and add them as the last step. Cook for 3-5 minutes on low heat to retain their color and nutrients.
    • Serve Hot: Enjoy your Oha Soup with fufu, pounded yam, or any preferred side!
